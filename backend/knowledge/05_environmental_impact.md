# Environmental Impact

## Climate Change and Carbon Emissions
Waste management is intrinsically linked to climate change. The collection, transport, and disposal of waste generate greenhouse gases. Landfills are a major source of anthropogenic methane.

## Methane Emissions from Landfills
When organic waste (food scraps, paper, yard trimmings) decomposes in a landfill under anaerobic (oxygen-free) conditions, it produces landfill gas, which is primarily composed of methane (CH4) and carbon dioxide (CO2). Methane is a potent greenhouse gas, approximately 28 to 36 times more effective at trapping heat in the atmosphere than CO2 over a 100-year period.

## Pollution
*   **Air Pollution:** Open burning of waste releases toxic chemicals, including dioxins and furans, which cause severe respiratory illnesses and environmental damage.
*   **Water Pollution:** Leachate—the toxic liquid that drains from a landfill—can contaminate groundwater and surface water supplies if landfills are not properly lined.
*   **Soil Degradation:** Hazardous chemicals from e-waste and industrial waste can render soil infertile and toxic.

## Plastic Pollution
Plastics take hundreds of years to degrade. They break down into microplastics, which infiltrate waterways, oceans, and the food chain. Marine biodiversity is severely threatened by plastic ingestion and entanglement.

## Biodiversity Impacts
Habitat destruction for landfill creation and the pollution of natural ecosystems directly threaten wildlife. Microplastics and toxins bioaccumulate up the food chain, ultimately affecting human health.
