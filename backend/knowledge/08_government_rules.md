# Government and Municipal Systems

## Municipal Waste Management Workflows
Municipal Solid Waste (MSW) management involves several stages:
1.  **Primary Collection:** Door-to-door collection by municipal workers or contracted agencies.
2.  **Secondary Collection:** Transfer of waste from primary collection vehicles to larger compactor trucks at transfer stations.
3.  **Processing/Treatment:** Segregation at Materials Recovery Facilities (MRFs), composting of wet waste, or waste-to-energy incineration.
4.  **Disposal:** Scientific landfilling of inert rejects.

## Smart City Systems
Modern municipalities use technology for efficient waste management:
*   GPS tracking of garbage trucks.
*   Fill-level sensors in public bins.
*   Citizen apps (like IWIS) for grievance redressal and hotspot reporting.
*   Data analytics for route optimization.

## Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM)
A country-wide campaign initiated by the Government of India to eliminate open defecation and improve solid waste management. It heavily emphasizes source segregation, door-to-door collection, and behavioral change.

## Key Waste Management Rules (India)
1.  **Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016:** Mandates source segregation into wet, dry, and domestic hazardous waste. Makes it the generator's responsibility to segregate.
2.  **Plastic Waste Management Rules:** Focuses on the phase-out of single-use plastics and introduces Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR).
3.  **E-Waste (Management) Rules:** Mandates that e-waste be channeled only to authorized dismantlers and recyclers.
4.  **Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR):** A policy approach under which producers are given a significant responsibility (financial and/or physical) for the treatment or disposal of post-consumer products.
