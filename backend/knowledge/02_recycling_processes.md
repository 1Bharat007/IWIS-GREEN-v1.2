# Recycling Knowledge Base

## The Recycling Process
Recycling is the process of collecting and processing materials that would otherwise be thrown away as trash and turning them into new products.

### Collection Process
Recyclables are collected through curbside collection, drop-off centers, and buy-back centers. In the IWIS platform, the **Circular Exchange** connects bulk generators directly with certified recyclers.

### Sorting Process
At a Materials Recovery Facility (MRF), recyclables are sorted using human labor and advanced machinery (screens, magnets, optical sorters, eddy currents) to separate paper, glass, plastics, and metals.

## Plastic Recycling
Not all plastics are equal. They are categorized by resin codes (1-7):
*   **PETE (1):** Water bottles, highly recyclable.
*   **HDPE (2):** Milk jugs, shampoo bottles, highly recyclable.
*   **PVC (3), LDPE (4), PP (5), PS (6), Other (7):** Varying degrees of recyclability; soft plastics often require special drop-off points.
**Process:** Plastics are sorted, washed, shredded into flakes, melted, and extruded into pellets to be sold to manufacturers.

## Paper Recycling
Paper is sorted by grade. It is mixed with water and chemicals to break it down into pulp. It is then screened, cleaned, de-inked, bleached, and rolled into new paper.
**Limits:** Paper fibers shorten each time they are recycled. Paper can typically be recycled 5-7 times.

## Metal Recycling
Metals (aluminum and steel) are infinitely recyclable without loss of quality.
**Process:** Metals are shredded, melted in a furnace, purified, and cast into blocks or sheets. Recycling aluminum saves 95% of the energy required to make it from raw bauxite ore.

## Glass Recycling
Glass is 100% recyclable and can be recycled endlessly.
**Process:** Glass is sorted by color, crushed into "cullet," cleaned, mixed with raw materials, and melted in a furnace to mold new bottles or fiberglass.

## E-waste Recycling
E-waste contains hazardous chemicals (lead, mercury, cadmium) but also valuable precious metals (gold, silver, copper).
**Process:** Devices are manually dismantled. Batteries and hazardous components are removed safely. Circuit boards and wires are shredded and chemically or thermally processed to extract precious metals.

## Textile & Construction Recycling
*   **Textiles:** Sorted by color and material. Usable clothes are donated. Unusable textiles are shredded for insulation or wiping rags.
*   **Construction Debris:** Concrete and brick are crushed to be used as road base. Wood is chipped for mulch or biomass energy.
