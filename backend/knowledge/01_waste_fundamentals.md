# Waste Management Fundamentals

## What is Waste
Waste is any material or substance that is discarded, has no further immediate use, or is required to be disposed of. Effective waste management is crucial for maintaining public health and environmental sustainability.

## Types of Waste
1. **Municipal Solid Waste (MSW):** Everyday items we use and then throw away, such as product packaging, grass clippings, furniture, clothing, bottles, food scraps, and newspapers.
2. **Organic Waste:** Biodegradable waste, such as food waste, green waste, paper waste, and biodegradable plastics.
3. **Plastic Waste:** Synthetic or semi-synthetic materials that are difficult to decompose. E.g., bottles, bags, wrappers.
4. **Paper Waste:** Discarded paper materials. Highly recyclable.
5. **Glass Waste:** Discarded glass bottles and jars. 100% recyclable without loss of quality.
6. **Metal Waste:** Scrap metal, tin cans, aluminum foils. Highly valuable and recyclable.
7. **Biomedical Waste:** Waste containing infectious materials, often generated by hospitals and clinics. Must be incinerated or autoclaved.
8. **Hazardous Waste:** Waste with properties that make it dangerous or capable of having a harmful effect on human health or the environment (e.g., chemicals, batteries, paints).
9. **Construction Waste:** Debris generated during the construction, renovation, and demolition of buildings, roads, and bridges.
10. **Industrial Waste:** Waste produced by industrial activity which includes any material that is rendered useless during a manufacturing process.
11. **Agricultural Waste:** Waste produced as a result of various agricultural operations. It includes manure and other wastes from farms, poultry houses, and slaughterhouses.
12. **Electronic Waste (E-waste):** Discarded electrical or electronic devices (phones, laptops, TVs).

## Segregation Methods
Effective segregation occurs at the source. The three-bin system is widely recommended:
*   **Green Bin:** Wet/Organic waste (food scraps, peels, leaves).
*   **Blue Bin:** Dry waste (plastic, paper, glass, metal).
*   **Red/Black Bin:** Reject/Hazardous/Sanitary waste (diapers, batteries, broken ceramics).

## Disposal Methods
*   **Recycling:** Converting waste materials into new materials and objects.
*   **Composting:** Natural biodegradation process that turns organic waste into nutrient-rich soil.
*   **Incineration:** Combustion of organic substances contained in waste materials (Waste-to-Energy).
*   **Landfills:** Burying waste in designated land areas. This is the least preferable method due to methane emissions and groundwater contamination.

## Environmental Impacts of Improper Management
Improper waste management leads to water, soil, and air pollution. It contributes heavily to greenhouse gas emissions (specifically methane from rotting organic waste in landfills) and causes severe harm to marine and terrestrial biodiversity (e.g., microplastics).
