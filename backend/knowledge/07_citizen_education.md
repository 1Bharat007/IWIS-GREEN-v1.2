# Citizen Education and Civic Sense

## Civic Sense and Public Cleanliness
Civic sense refers to the unspoken norms of society that keep it running smoothly without friction. In the context of waste, it means taking personal responsibility for the cleanliness of public spaces, parks, and streets. Littering is a failure of civic sense.

## Responsible Waste Disposal
*   Never throw trash out of moving vehicles.
*   If a bin is not available, carry the wrapper/waste until you find one.
*   Do not dump waste in open lots, rivers, or drains.
*   Always participate in community clean-up drives.

## Waste Segregation at Source
Segregation at source is the most critical step in waste management. Mixed waste has no value and ends up in landfills.
*   **Rule:** Keep wet waste (organic) separate from dry waste (recyclables) and sanitary waste.
*   Rinse food containers before putting them in the dry waste bin to prevent contamination.

## Household Sustainability Practices
*   **Reduce:** Buy in bulk to reduce packaging. Say no to single-use plastics.
*   **Reuse:** Use cloth bags, glass jars, and durable containers instead of disposable ones.
*   **Recycle:** Use the IWIS scanner to identify recyclables and ensure they go to the right bin.
*   **Compost:** Start a small compost bin for vegetable peels and coffee grounds.

## Common Misconceptions
*   *Myth:* "All plastic goes to the same place anyway." *Fact:* Segregated plastics are highly valuable and are actively sought by recyclers.
*   *Myth:* "Biodegradable plastic is harmless if littered." *Fact:* Most biodegradable plastics only break down in industrial composting facilities, not in natural environments.
