# Food Waste Management

## The Food Waste Problem
Roughly one-third of all food produced globally for human consumption is lost or wasted. This not only represents a massive economic loss but also a significant environmental crisis, as rotting food in landfills generates methane, a potent greenhouse gas.

## Causes of Food Waste
*   **Household:** Over-purchasing, improper storage, confusion over "best before" vs. "use by" dates, discarding leftovers.
*   **Commercial (Restaurants/Catering):** Over-preparation, large portion sizes, buffet surplus.
*   **Events (Weddings/Banquets):** Huge surplus due to overestimation of guest consumption.
*   **Supply Chain:** Spoilage during transport, aesthetic rejection by supermarkets ("ugly produce").

## IWIS Food Rescue Network & Donation Guidelines
The IWIS platform supports the redistribution of surplus food from commercial entities and large events to those in need.

### Wedding and Event Food Waste
Bulk food generators (wedding halls, large banquets) can use IWIS to signal surplus food.
**Workflow:**
1. Event organizer reports surplus food volume and type on the IWIS app before the event ends.
2. Verified NGO partners receive instant alerts based on proximity.
3. NGOs arrange pickup via cold-chain transport.
**Safety Requirement:** Food must be stored at safe temperatures (hot food above 60°C, cold food below 5°C) and must not have been served to plates.

### Restaurant and Supermarket Support
*   **Surplus Sales:** Restaurants can list near-expiry or surplus end-of-day meals at a discount.
*   **Donation:** Unsold, safe inventory can be automatically routed to local orphanages or shelters.

### NGO Food Redistribution
NGOs partnering with IWIS must maintain FSSAI (or local equivalent) food safety compliance. They act as the bridge between surplus generators and marginalized communities.

### Animal Feed and Gaushala Integration
Food that is no longer fit for human consumption (e.g., vegetable trimmings, stale bread) but is still safe for animals can be routed to:
*   **Gaushalas (Cow Shelters):** Safe vegetable waste.
*   **Pig Farms / Poultry:** Processed food scraps.
*   *Note:* Food must be strictly segregated from plastics, toothpicks, and bones to prevent harm to animals.

### Black Soldier Fly (BSF) Farming
Severely spoiled food waste can be fed to Black Soldier Fly larvae, which rapidly consume organic waste and are then harvested as high-protein feed for poultry and aquaculture.
